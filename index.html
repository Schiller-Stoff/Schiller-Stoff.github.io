I'm a Digital Humanist specialised in software quality assurance, research software engineering and digital archiving. I believe that we need more than trivial measurements to understand the reliability of our increasingly complex research software and data in science. Without correctly applied testing strategies our intepretations will be negatively affected by overly-optimistic assumptions about the - often only manually reviewed - reliability of our algorithms. This especially counts for software producing unstable and not-reproducible results.

Since 2017 I have been working as a (web) developer and now as a research assistant at the University of Graz. In the context of the Cantus Network project ("a semantically enriched digital edition of libri ordinarii of the Salzburg metropolitan province") I gained expertise in the deep digital exploration and scientific evaluation of (liturgical) historical source material. Within the DERLA project - a cooperation with the Center for Jewish Studies Graz - I am responsible for the digital long-term archiving and processing of research data as well as for their digital-geographical analysis. I graduated in history, art history and digital humanities at the University of Graz.
